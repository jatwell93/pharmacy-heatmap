## PART 3 ŌĆö VISUAL IDENTITY

### 3.1 Brand Rationale: Why These Choices

**Why Teal, not Blue or Green alone?**
Every competitor in Australian pharmacy tech defaults to blue (clinical trust) or green (health/pharmacy cross). Teal occupies the intersection ŌĆö it inherits the trust of blue and the health associations of green, while being visually distinct. Paired with Australian amber/gold, it creates a palette that is unmistakably healthcare-professional without blending into the sea of blue competitors.

**Why Amber?**
Amber/ochre has strong visual associations with the Australian landscape ŌĆö the ochre of the outback, the warmth of the sun. In UI terms it functions as an alert/attention color (warnings, expiry flags, dead-stock notices) that aligns naturally with its urgency function. It also creates warmth and approachability against the cooler teal.

**Why Plus Jakarta Sans + Inter?**
Plus Jakarta Sans brings geometric confidence and a premium SaaS quality to headings. Inter is the gold standard for screen legibility in data-heavy UIs. Together they create a typographic hierarchy that feels modern and intelligent without clinical stiffness.

### 3.3 Typography

PharmIQ uses a **two-tier typography system** ŌĆö one for B2B platform products (pharmacy owner tools) and one for consumer-facing worker tools. Both share the teal brand and colour palette; the fonts are tuned to their respective audiences.

> **DEFAULT:** When in doubt, use **Space Grotesk + Inter**. This is the correct choice for all platform products, website, marketing, decks, and social content. Only switch to Bricolage Grotesque + DM Sans when the product is explicitly targeting workers/employees (e.g. Pay Checker). If you're not sure, default to Space Grotesk + Inter.

#### Platform Products (Dashboard, Expiry Mate, B2B SaaS)

Audience: pharmacy owners, managers, multi-site operators ŌĆö expert users making business decisions.

```css
--font-heading: 'Space Grotesk', 'Sora', system-ui, sans-serif;
--font-body:    'Inter', system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
--font-data:    'Inter', 'Tabular Figures', system-ui, sans-serif;
--font-mono:    'JetBrains Mono', 'Fira Code', monospace;
```

**Why Space Grotesk?**
Competitive research confirmed XTender uses Plus Jakarta Sans ŌĆö the most common premium-SaaS font choice in the category. Space Grotesk has a subtly distinct character: slightly condensed, with quirky optical details in letters like 'a', 'e', and 'G' that give it personality without sacrificing professionalism. It's used by Plaid, Linear, and Vercel. Available free on Google Fonts. Pairs excellently with Inter for body.

**Load Order (Google Fonts):**


#### Consumer / Worker Tools (Pay Checker and similar)

Audience: pharmacy, retail, and hospitality workers ŌĆö often young, often on mobile, checking their own pay after a shift. Potentially stressed. The tool must feel like it's on their side, not like corporate HR software.

```css
--font-heading: 'Bricolage Grotesque', system-ui, sans-serif;
--font-body:    'DM Sans', system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
```

**Why Bricolage Grotesque?**
Space Grotesk reads as "professional SaaS" ŌĆö correct for pharmacy owners, but slightly cold for workers checking if they've been underpaid. Bricolage Grotesque is a variable-weight font with an optical size axis, giving it more warmth and visual personality at bold display weights without sacrificing legibility. It's distinctive, approachable, and confident ŌĆö exactly the tone a worker advocacy tool should project.

**Why DM Sans?**
Inter is the gold standard for dense data UIs. DM Sans has a warmer, more humanist quality that suits consumer-facing apps where the user isn't a professional analyst. Same legibility, softer character.

## PART 4B ŌĆö CONSUMER PRODUCT DESIGN CONTEXT

> This section documents design principles for PharmIQ products targeting workers (employees) rather than pharmacy owners or managers. The primary product in this category is the **Pay Checker** ŌĆö a tool workers use to verify they are being paid correctly under their Modern Award.

### Consumer Audience Profile

**Who:** Pharmacy, retail, and hospitality workers ŌĆö often young (18ŌĆō30), frequently on mobile, checking their pay after a shift or at the end of a week.

**Context of use:** Potentially stressful. They suspect underpayment and are looking for confirmation. The tool must feel trustworthy, clear, and on their side ŌĆö not like employer-facing HR software.

**Device:** Mobile-first. Assume small screen, one hand, limited attention.

**Emotional goal:** The user should leave feeling confident and informed ŌĆö not confused or intimidated by award complexity.

---

### Consumer Design Principles

These five principles govern all design decisions on consumer-facing worker tools:

1. **Clarity over decoration** ŌĆö Every typographic and layout choice must make data easier to read, not just more beautiful. If in doubt, remove decoration.

2. **Hierarchy through contrast** ŌĆö Headings must be unmistakably different from body text in weight, size, and family. Dense pay data demands strong visual scaffolding.

3. **Readable at 16px minimum** ŌĆö Body text, form labels, and interactive controls must all be Ōēź16px. Workers reading on mobile after a shift need comfortable text ŌĆö and 16px prevents iOS input zoom.

4. **Warm but precise** ŌĆö Rounded, humanist letterforms (Bricolage Grotesque + DM Sans) feel approachable. Tabular numerics keep pay data accurate and aligned. Both matter equally.

5. **Mobile-first** ŌĆö Type scale, spacing, and touch targets are designed for small screens first. Larger viewports inherit from the mobile base, not the other way around.

## PART 7 ŌĆö COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION

### 7.1 Market Context (Research-Backed)

| Competitor | Primary Color | Tone | Gap PharmIQ Fills |
|-----------|--------------|------|-------------------|
| **MedAdvisor** | Deep blue `#0056E0` + purple | Corporate, patient-first | No pharmacy operations/profit focus |
| **Z Software** | Dark charcoal + cyan/purple | Conversational, anti-legacy | No analytics depth; limited data intelligence |
| **Pharmacy Hive** | Black + teal, glassmorphism | Practical, compliance-focused | Compliance-only; no commercial intelligence |
| **Dragatron** | Dark slate `#0F172A` + cloud blue | Technical, AI-heavy | Enterprise PMS pricing; no free tier |
| **POSWorks** | Pure greyscale `#222` | Utilitarian, no-frills | Aged UI; no analytics or intelligence layer |
| **XTender** | Blue `#1565C0` + orange | Direct, pharmacist-first | Narrow toolset; no multi-site data network |
| **Pharmacy Halo** | Teal `#39C0C0` + orange | Warm, module-based | Patient-focused modules; no revenue/stock intelligence |

**Key sector-wide gaps (confirmed by research):**
- 6 of 7 competitors use blue as primary ŌĆö teal at `#0F766E` (darker, more serious) is unoccupied
- Pharmacy Halo uses lighter teal `#39C0C0` ŌĆö meaningfully different from PharmIQ's deeper tone
- "Streamline operations" and "built by pharmacists" are now commodity claims ŌĆö avoid both
- Multi-site pharmacy networks are underserved in messaging and feature design
- "Book a Demo" is the universal CTA ŌĆö PharmIQ should use outcome-oriented CTAs
- No competitor leads with **commercial outcomes** (profit, margins, revenue per script)

### 7.2 PharmIQ's Visual Differentiators

1. **Deep Teal `#0F766E`** ŌĆö Occupies uncontested colour territory; distinct from Pharmacy Halo's lighter `#39C0C0`; not the default blue of every other competitor
2. **Amber `#D97706` accent** ŌĆö No competitor uses warm amber/gold; distinctly Australian; functionally correct for urgent alerts
3. **Two-tier heading system** ŌĆö Space Grotesk for B2B platform products (XTender uses Plus Jakarta Sans ŌĆö we remain unique); Bricolage Grotesque for consumer/worker tools (warmer, more approachable, still distinctive across the full competitive set)
4. **Commercial-first voice** ŌĆö Every competitor leads with operations/admin/compliance relief; PharmIQ leads with margin and revenue outcomes
5. **Data-forward hero design** ŌĆö Dashboards shown as hero imagery (not stock photos); the product is the visual proof
6. **"Mate" product naming** ŌĆö Expiry Mate establishes uniquely Australian casual-professional product identity; aligns with how Australian pharmacists actually talk
7. **Free tier genuinely marketed** ŌĆö No competitor offers or prominently features a free analytics tier; this is a major acquisition advantage
